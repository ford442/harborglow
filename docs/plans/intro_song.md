# HarborGlow Intro — Lyric & Style Analysis

## Original Song Context

This is a lyrical pastiche of "I Can See Clearly Now" (Johnny Nash) adapted for
Harborglow, a crane-operator + boat equipment upgrade game with neon harbor
aesthetics, shaders, and glowing digital tides. The trance treatment at 140 BPM
will push these lyrics hard — any phonetic clumsiness gets magnified.

## Critical Singability Issues Found

| # | Problem | Where | Why It Fails |
|---|---------|-------|--------------|
| 1 | "obstacles" | Verse 1 | 3 syllables with schwa + final /lz/ cluster. Gets mushy at 140 BPM. |
| 2 | "It's gonna be a bright bright" | Verses + Drop | Bilabial pile-up: /ɡʌnə bi ə braɪt braɪt/. Five /b/ attacks in 6 words. Tongue twister. |
| 3 | "It's going to be" | Drop (lines 4-7) | Switches from "gonna" → "going to". /ɡoʊɪŋ tu/ is 3 syllables of rapid tongue motion; gets devoured by sidechain compression. Also inconsistent. |
| 4 | "yeah now" tacked on | Drop line 5 | 13 syllables crammed into an 8-beat phrase. "dayyeahnow" becomes one blur. |
| 5 | "bright bright bright bright bright" (x5) | Drop + Outro | /br/ requires lips-to-teeth transition every time. Vocal fatigue on 4th-5th rep. 5 = odd phrase length (music wants 4 or 8). |
| 6 | "Bright sunshiny… digital day!" | Final Build | "sunshiny" (4 syll) → "digital" (3 syll) meter mismatch. /dʒ/ in "digital" interrupts warm nostalgic feel. |
| 7 | "Shaders pulse and the beats" | Bridge | "and the" = /æn ðə/. /nð/ cluster is hard to sing; filler words anyway. |
| 8 | "code we rise" | Bridge | /d/ → /w/ transition. Hard stop into round lip vowel. Slight hiccup at half-time tempo. |

## Quick Reference — Why These Words Are Hard to Sing

**"obstacles" → "barriers"**
- obstacles: /'ɒbstəkəlz/ — 3 syllables, schwa, /stək/ cluster, /lz/ ending
- barriers: /'bæriərz/ — 3 syllables but cleaner vowels, no schwa in the stressed syllable

**"It's going to" → "It's gonna"**
- going to: /ɡoʊɪŋ tu/ — 3 syllables, diphthong + nasal + high vowel. Hard to punch through a dense trance mix.
- gonna: /ɡʌnə/ — 2 syllables, schwa release, sits better in the pocket.

**"bright" x5**
- /braɪt/: bilabial onset /br/ means lips-to-teeth transition on every attack.
- At 140 BPM, 5 reps in 2 beats = 10ms per transition. Vocalists stumble.
- Fix: 4 reps + open vowel "whoa" gives lip muscles a reset.

**"digital" → "neon"**
- digital: /'dɪdʒɪtəl/ — affricate /dʒ/ (hard "j") breaks the warm, sunshiny flow.
- neon: /'niːɒn/ — 2 syllables, all continuant sounds, matches "sunshiny" meter perfectly.
